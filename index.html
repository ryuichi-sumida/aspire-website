Pillars of Inclusive Science

Linguistic Inclusion

Acknowledging sign languages, tactile languages, and multimodal repertoires as full and legitimate scientific resources.

Interactional Inclusion

Valuing handshape, gaze, touch, and spatial coordination as forms of reasoning and theorizing.

Epistemic Inclusion

Involving Deaf communities in defining research problems, interpreting data, and shaping frameworks.

Technological Inclusion

Designing AI and corpus tools that respect the structural specificities of sign and tactile interaction.

Institutional Inclusion

Establishing structures where people are co-researchers, advisors, and decision-makers.
